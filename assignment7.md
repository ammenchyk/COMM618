I created a pivot table from the data provided via OpenDataDC to display the numbers. The data shows the total number of “vehicles” that were involved in an accident in 2025, and this is all broken down by ward. I use vehicles in quotation as pedestrians are not vehicles but for sake of simplicity, they will be included in the vehicle terminology. Government vehicles are separated from the vehicles section, as well as taxis. These groups both received their own listings as they are not civilian vehicles. Taxis were by far the lowest number involved in accidents in 2025. On the opposite end, civilian vehicles were by far the highest number involved in accidents. 

Ward 2 showed to have the highest number of "vehicles" that were involved in accidents. With Ward 3 having nearly 5,000 less than Ward 2. The difference shows that there is some sort of inconsistency between the Wards, whether there are stricter road rules or there is more traffic, there is a clear difference. With this in mind, looking into what is in the wards could also explain why there is such a large difference. Are there more businesses or homes? 


What I found:
I found that creating this pivot table helped me to understand the data a little bit easier. It was easier to see the breakdown by vehicle per ward. For example, it helped me to see what ward had the most taxi accidents in 2025. I thought this was an insightful way to display the breakdown of the data. I might suggest leaving it in a table format for an article because of how the data is broken down. I believe that it might be the easiest to understand the data that way.
