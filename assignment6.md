This dataset displays the amount of fatalities across Washington, D.C. in 2025 and is broken down by wards. There has been a recorded total of twenty-one traffic accident fatalities by ward in 2025. This data is displayed in a bar chart to accurately, and easily, display this data. From initial observation, there is a clear question of why there is no data for ward 3. While ward 3 had traffic accidents, just like the other wards, there were no fatalities. This is vastly different from ward 8, which had the highest amount of fatalities which was six. This data shows the vast difference in traffic accidents within the same city. This breakdown could present what wards may be busier or even just having safer roads. 

This data presents itself to be trustworthy through what sourced the data. I had found this data on the website OpenDataDC. This website had stated that the data was sourced to them from the DC traffic and safety website. Since this data is sourced through an official government entity, it should be factual and trustworthy. 

This data is only part of a bigger possible story. It shows that there is a clear discrepancy in wards throughout D.C. There is a clear show of wards that are safer to drive in and walk in compared to others that are not as safe. This could be related to traffic, traffic signs, and/or general population. This smaller dataset is only the catalysis. Looking at more data provided via OpenDataDC would help answer some of the questions surrounding the large imbalance in the data. Whether or not this data is truly related to what the traffic and safety department can do, there is a clear show that something is not the same between the wards.
